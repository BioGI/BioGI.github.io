Validation of new particle dissolution model using Yanxing data

Farhad Behafarid, Ganesh Vijayakumar
25 Mar 2016

Chosen case from Yanxing’s data files

One of the Yanxing’s simulations was chosen as defined by the parameters below: \[ \begin{equation*} \left.\begin{aligned} U_o = 0.1 m/s \\ S^* = 1 \\ Re_s= 0.1 \\ \nu= 0.125 m^2/s \\ \end{aligned} \right\} \qquad \text{corresponding to [re01s-b/sca-*-01.dat]} \end{equation*} \] The output plots are presented in Figures below

Parameters

Using below equations we can get the dimensional parameters needed for our simulations: \[ \begin{equation*} \left.\begin{aligned} S = \frac{U_o}{H} \\ Re_s = \frac{S \; R^2}{ \nu} \\ S^* = Re_s \; Sc \\ H/R = 20\\ L/R = 50 \\ W/R = 20 \\ \end{aligned} \right\} \qquad \text{Non-dimensional parameters} \end{equation*} \] \[ \begin{equation*} \left.\begin{aligned} R = \frac{20 \; Re_s \; \nu }{U_o} =2.5 m\\ H = W = 20 R = 50 m \\ L = 50 R = 125 m \\ Sc = \frac{S*}{Re_s} = 10\\ D_m = \frac{\nu}{Sc} = 0.0125 m^2/s\\ C_s =1\\ \end{aligned} \right\} \qquad \text{Dimensional parameters} \end{equation*} \]

Sherwood number based on Figure (2)

We have \(N^"_s A_P\) from Figure (2):

\[ \begin{equation*} \begin{split} N^"_s A_P \approx 4 \\ A_P= 4 \pi R^2= 4 \pi (2.5)^2 =78.54 m^2 \\ N^"_s= \frac{4} {78.54} \approx 0.05 \frac{mols} {m^2.s} \end{split} \end{equation*} \]

based on Figure (3), we can assume \(C_b = 0\), therefore: \[ \begin{equation*} \begin{split} Sh= \frac{N^"_s} {D_m (\frac{C_s-C_b}{R}) } = \frac{0.05} {0.0125 (\frac{1-0}{2.5})} = 10 \\ \end{split} \end{equation*} \] The estimated Sherwood number of 10 is much larger than the value we expected (Sh=1.3 from Sherwood plots and correlations)
