How to implement the dissolution model in GI code

Dissolution from small particles in numerical simulation

Mmagnitude ranges for particle and lattice cell sizes

\[ \begin{equation*} \begin{split} \forall_{\Delta}= \Delta^3=cell \hskip {0.1in} volume \\ \Delta \sim (100-1000) \mu m \\ R_j \sim (10-50) \mu m \\ \dfrac{R_j}{\Delta} \sim (0.01-0.5) < 1 \\ \end{split} \end{equation*} \]

Basic LBM equations

\[ \begin{equation*} \begin{split} \phi_i = concentration \\ \dfrac{dN_{b_j}}{dt}=q''_{s_j} A_{s_j} \\ \end{split} \end{equation*} \]

Where: \[ \begin{equation*} Sh_j = {\dfrac{R}{\delta}}_j = \dfrac{q''_s}{D_m \big( \dfrac{C_s-C_b}{R}\big)}=1+\Delta_{con} + \Delta_{hyd} \end{equation*} \] And, \[ \begin{equation*} \begin{split} r_j = | \vec{x}- \vec{x}_j | \\ C(r_j)= ( C_s - C_{{\infty}_j} ) \dfrac{R_j}{r_j} + C_{{\infty}_j} \\ C_{{\infty}_j} = \dfrac{{C_b}_j-\gamma {C_s}_j}{1-\gamma_j} \\ \gamma_j=f(\forall/\forall_C)_j \\ \end{split} \end{equation*} \] Therefore for \(\gamma=0\), we get \(C_{{\infty}_j}=C_b\).

The basic LBM is as follows: \[ \begin{equation*} \dfrac{\partial \phi(\vec{x},t)}{\partial{t}} +\nabla . (\vec{u} \phi) = \nabla . \vec{q}''_m \end{equation*} \] Where \(\vec{q}''_m = - D_m \nabla \phi=\) local mole flux.

Continuum Point Particle Model

Models release of molecules \(dN_b/dt\) from points at location of particles: \[ \begin{equation*} \dfrac{\partial \phi(\vec{x},t)}{\partial{t}} +\nabla . (\vec{u} \phi) = S(\vec{x},t) + \nabla . \vec{q}''_m \end{equation*} \] Where, \[ \begin{equation*} S(\vec{x},t)=\dfrac{d \phi_s(\vec{x},t)}{dt}, \end{equation*} \] is the rate of change in local concentration field due to release of the molecules from particle j, or in other words, “source” to \(\phi(\vec{x},t)\) in local volume \(\delta \forall_j\)
